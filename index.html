Bournemouth Festival Application Process

This is not an official document of the Bournemouth Festival Music Competion, this is simply a set of steps to help with the applciation process. The aim is to introduce more people to this opportunity of celebrating music. This guide has been set up using the festival's website at bmcf.info

The (extended) deadline for the Instrumental section of the 2023 Bournemouth Festival is 26th March

  1. Choose and order your piece
    • Pieces must be for the grade you are currently studying for, and can be any of the 30 provided by following the appropriate ABRSM syllabus prompts
    • You can copy the name of each piece and pasted it into youtube to listen to the first few bars
    • Pieces can be ordered from the above link
  2. Complete the 2023 entry form entry form
    • Classes can found in the appropriate section of the syllabus
  3. Send the form. This can either be done by:
    • scanning / photographing and emailing to the address on the form
    • posting to the address provided on the form
  4. Send payment. This can either be done by:
    • including a cheque with your posted form, made payable to 'Bournemouth Music Competitions Festival'
    • making a bank transfer to the details provided on the form (NB: Ensure that you reference matches the one your provided on the form)
  5. You will also need the following to hand in on the day:
    • Own Choice Form
    • A photocopy of your music
  6. Check for confirmation
    • You should receive an email confirming receipt of your application and payment, which will complete the application process. If you do not hear back within a week, you can enquire here using the contact form, or via the telephone number at the bottom of the page
  7. Arrange an accompanist. You can:
    • Use an accompanist that you already know, (most common)
    • Book an accompanist (at a price?)
    • Use a non vocal backing track, in accordance with the festival rules of the syllabus. Conditions for backing tracks are outlined in the festival rules, but feel free to contact them if you have any questions.
  8. Look out for the timetable
    • A couple weeks after the application closing date, the timetable will be posted. This will show the time and order of your class
  9. Understand more about the festival
    • I strongly encourage you to read through the syllabus. There are many other important things which have not been included in this guide.
