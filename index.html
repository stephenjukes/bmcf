Bournemouth Festival Application Process

This is not an official document of the Bournemouth Festival Music Competion, it is simply a set of steps that I have put together to help make the application process more accessible. The aim is to introduce more people to this opportunity of celebrating music and has been set up using the festival's website at bmcf.info

The 2023 instrumental classes are being held on the 22nd / 23rd April, and the (extended) deadline is 26th March

  1. Choose and order your piece
    • Pieces must be from a grade that you studied, are studying or will be studying within the current academic year, (ie: between last September and next August) [1] and can be any of the 30 provided by following the appropriate ABRSM syllabus prompts
    • You can copy the name of each piece and paste it into youtube to listen to the first few bars
    • Pieces can also be ordered from the above link
  2. Complete the 2023 entry form
    • Class numbers can found in the appropriate section of the syllabus
  3. Send the form. This can either be done by:
    • scanning / photographing and emailing to the address on the form
    • posting to the address provided on the form
  4. Send payment. This can either be done by:
    • including a cheque with your posted form, made payable to 'Bournemouth Music Competitions Festival'
    • making a bank transfer to the details provided on the form (NB: Ensure that your reference matches the one you provided on the form)
  5. You will also need the following to hand in on the day:
    • Own Choice Form
    • A photocopy of your music
  6. Check for confirmation
    • You should receive an email confirming receipt of your form and payment, which will complete the application process. If you do not hear back within a week, you can enquire here using the contact form, or via the telephone number at the bottom of the page
  7. Arrange an accompanist. You can:
    • Use an accompanist that you already know, (most common)
    • Book an accompanist (at a price?)
    • Use a non vocal backing track [1]
  8. Look out for the timetable
    • A couple weeks after the application closing date, the timetable will be posted. This will show the time and order of your class
  9. Understand more about the festival
    • I strongly encourage you to read through the syllabus which includes details for each of the above points, as well as other important information not included in this guide.
